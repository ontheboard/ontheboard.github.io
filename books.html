Books on chess

This page gives brief details (title and author) about a few chess books of historic importance, most of which are still available today, either from Dover Publications, Russell Enterprises, Ishi, or Edition Olms. Newer books are also available from some of these, as well as Gambit and New in Chess.

Chess classics

These were originally published in descriptive notation. For example, 'e2-e4' would be described as 'king's pawn to king's fourth'. The Dover Chess series from Dover Publications still makes them available in the old descriptive notation. The main modern publishers of them are Dover Publications, Russell Enterprises, Ishi, and Edition Olms. I believe that Russell Enterprises and Ishi have been updating some of them to use algebraic notation.

  • The Art of Attack in Chess - Vladimir Vuković (1963)
  • Modern Chess Strategy - Luděk Pachman (1959)
  • The Game of Chess - Siegbert Tarrash (1935)
  • Masters of the Chess Board - Richard Réti (1933)
  • Lasker's Manual of Chess - Emanuel Lasker (1927)
  • Lehrbuch des Schachspiels - Emanuel Lasker (1926)
  • My System / Mein System - Aron Nimzowitsch (1925)
  • Chess Fundamentals - José Raúl Capablanca (1921/1934)
  • The Modern Chess Instructor - Wilhelm Steinitz (1889)
  • The Chess-Player's Handbook - Howard Staunton (1847)
  • Handbuch des Schachspiels - von der Lasa (1843)
  • Analysis of the Game of Chess / Analyse du jeu des Échecs- François-André Danican Philidor (1749)
  • Libro de la invención liberal y Arte del juego del Axedrez - Ruy Lopez de Segura (1561)
